Buying a home

  1. Sort your finance and get pre-approved.
  2. Set your must-haves, suburbs and budget.
  3. Attend open homes and do your due diligence (LIM, title, builder's report).
  4. Understand the sale method — auction, negotiation, deadline or tender.
  5. Make your offer and negotiate with confidence.
  6. Go unconditional, settle, and collect the keys.

Selling your home

  1. Get a current, evidence-based appraisal.
  2. Choose your sale method and timing.
  3. Prepare and present the home (declutter, repairs, styling).
  4. Professional photography and a strong marketing plan.
  5. Open homes, buyer feedback and weekly updates.
  6. Negotiate the best result and manage to settlement.

Frequently asked questions

How much is my home worth?

The best way to find out is a current appraisal based on recent comparable sales in your area. Request a free, no-obligation appraisal and Garry will prepare one for you.

What does it cost to work with an agent?

Selling fees and marketing costs are agreed in writing before you list, in your agency agreement. Garry will explain every cost clearly up front — no surprises.

Auction, negotiation or deadline sale — which is best?

It depends on your property, the market and your goals. Garry will talk you through the pros and cons of each method for your situation.

Do I need a lawyer?

Yes — both buyers and sellers should engage a property lawyer or conveyancer to handle the legal side and review documents before you sign.
